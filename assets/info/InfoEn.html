About the CO₂ App

Version: 3.3 (upcoming: 3.4)

Application Information

The CO₂ application is an educational tool that includes a knowledge quiz and a calculator for estimating the household carbon footprint in a simple yet reliable way, based on everyday activities.

Knowledge Quiz

The knowledge quiz helps users evaluate their understanding of CO₂ emissions, their sources, and their impact on the environment. The quiz has an educational purpose and includes explanations to support learning and awareness.

Key Assumptions

  • Internationally accepted CO₂ emission factors are used for different activities.
  • Emissions are calculated by category: Energy, Transportation, Food & Consumption.
  • Users provide estimates based on their habits.
  • Emissions are expressed on an annual basis (kg or tons of CO₂/year).

Calculation Method

The app multiplies estimated activities (such as electricity use or travel distance) by the corresponding CO₂ emission factors. The total footprint results from the sum of each category. Values are based on sources such as the UN, OECD, or the ADEME database.

Explanation of Factors

  • Base_Heating_kWh: Estimated annual energy consumption for heating (kWh).
  • CIheating: Carbon intensity factor for the heating fuel used (kg CO₂/kWh).
  • Housing_Factor: Reflects the size and type of the home.
  • Insulation_Factor: Represents the quality of thermal insulation.
  • Heating_Factor: Efficiency and technology of the heating system.
  • Base_Use_kWh: Annual electricity consumption for uses other than heating.
  • CIelectricity: Carbon intensity of electricity production, representing the amount of CO₂ generated per kWh (considering lifecycle emissions). For Greece, the average value for 2024 is approximately 0.326 kg CO₂/kWh.
  • Behavior_Factoruse: Factor related to consumer habits and behavioral patterns (e.g., frugal, average, excessive use).
