CO2App – Model documentation (v17)

CO2App is a PWA that estimates annual personal carbon footprint (in tCO₂/year) using user inputs grouped into: Housing, Transport, and Lifestyle.

The app is educational: it prioritizes order-of-magnitude understanding, comparative reasoning, and transparency (all constants and factors are stored in a central JSON). The model is tuned for Greece (especially southern Greece), which gives it local validity.

Basic structure

The total annual footprint is computed as:

CO2_total = CO2_housing + CO2_transport + CO2_lifestyle

Housing

Housing includes: (a) space heating, (b) household electricity excluding heating, (c) domestic hot water (DHW), and (d) per-occupant allocation.

CO2_homeElec = HomeElec_kWh × CI / 1000

CO2_housing = (CO2_heating + CO2_homeElec + CO2_DHW) / occupants

Transport

Land transport is reported as km/week and split into private vs public. For private vehicles an occupancy factor is applied.

CO2_road = (km_private × EF_private × OccFactor + km_public × EF_public) × 52 / 1000

Flights (educational reference distances). The model uses typical one-way distances d_GR (domestic) and d_EU (within Europe) to avoid false precision and to keep comparisons meaningful.

CO2_flights = (N_GR × d_GR × EF_flight,GR + N_EU × d_EU × EF_flight,EU) / 1000

Lifestyle

Lifestyle captures emissions from dietary patterns, goods consumption, and digital services. It highlights that consumption habits can be as important as energy and mobility.

CO2_diet = FoodBase × DietFactor / 1000

CO2_goods = GoodsUnit × GoodsProfileFactor / 1000

CO2_digital = DigitalUnit × DigitalLevelFactor / 1000

Social share (fixed overhead)

A fixed “social share” represents emissions not controlled by individual choices (public infrastructure/services). This also reinforces the model’s local validity.

CO2_social = SocialShare / 1000

Reference target (2030)

Reference target: 2.5 tCO₂/person/year.

Limits and assumptions

  • Not a certification tool.
  • No full product/service LCA.
  • No offsets/credits.
